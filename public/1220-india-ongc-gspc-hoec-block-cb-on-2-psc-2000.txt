ARTICLE - 2<br /><br />
DURATION<br /><br />
2.1<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The term of this Contract, subject to the terms hereof and the applicable law, shall be for a period of twenty five (25) years<br /><br />
from the Effcctive Date unless the Contract is terminated earlier in accordance with its terms. The period of Contract may be<br /><br />
extended for a further period of five (5) years, on same terms and conditions as set out in this agreement upon mutual<br /><br />
agreement by the Parties. Provided however, that in the event of Commercial Production of Non Associated Natural Gas,<br /><br />
the Contract may by mutual agreement between Parties be extended for a period upto but not exceeding thirty five (35) years<br /><br />
from the Effective Date.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.2<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The Exploration Period shall begin on the Effective Date and shall consist of three (3) exploration phases for a total period<br /><br />
not exceeding seven (7) consecutive Contract Years unless extended pursuant to the terms of this Contract.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.3<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Except as otherwise provided in this Contract, the term of the first exploration phase shall be one and one half (1.5)<br /><br />
consecutive Contract Years (hereinafter referred to as Phase I).<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.4<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Except as otherwise provided in this Contract, the term of the second exploration phase shall be three (3) consecutive<br /><br />
Contract Years from the end of Phase I (hereinafter referred to as Phase II).<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.5<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Except as otherwise provided in this Contract, the term of the third exploration phase shall be two and one half (2.5)<br /><br />
consecutive Contract Years from the end of Phase II (hereinafter referred to as Phase III).<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.6<br /><br />
<br /><br />
At the expiry of any exploration phase of the Exploration Period, provided that the Contractor has completed the minimum<br /><br />
agreed Work Programme for that exploration phase, the Contractor shall have the option, exercisable by giving written<br /><br />
notice to the Government at least thirty (30) days prior to the expiry of the relevant phase, either:<br /><br />
<br /><br />
(a)<br /><br />
<br /><br />
to terminate the Contract without obligation in respect of any subsequent phases of the Exploration Period; or<br /><br />
<br /><br />
(b)<br /><br />
<br /><br />
to proceed to the next exploration phase on presentation with the notice referred to above of the relevant guarantee as<br /><br />
provided for in Article 29; or<br /><br />
<br /><br />
(c)<br /><br />
<br /><br />
to relinquish the entire Contract Area except for any Discovery Area and any Development Area and to conduct<br /><br />
Development Operations and Production Operations in relation to any Discovery in accordance with the terms of this<br /><br />
Contract.<br /><br />
If neither of the options provided for in paragraphs (b) and (c) is exercised by the Contractor, this Contract shall terminate at<br /><br />
the end of the then current exploration phase.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.7<br /><br />
<br /><br />
If, at the end of any exploration phase, drilling or testing operations are in progress on a Well, included in a minimum<br /><br />
Work Programme, and the exploration phase expires, such exploration phase may, subject to prior approval of<br /><br />
Management Committee be extended for a period not cxceeding six (6) months and the period of such extension will be<br /><br />
subtracted from the period of the next succeeding exploration phase.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.8<br /><br />
<br /><br />
If, at the end of any exploration phase, drilling or testing operations are in progress on a Well not included in the minimum<br /><br />
Work Programme, such exploration phase may be extended for a period not exceeding six (6) months provided that the<br /><br />
minimum work commitment for such phase has been completed or the Licensee gives its consent to the said extension.<br /><br />
Such extension period shall be subtracted from the next succeeding exploration phase. In the event of an extension as<br /><br />
provided for herein, the notice referred to in Article 2.6 shall be given at least thirty (30) days prior to the expiry of the<br /><br />
relevant extension.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.9<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Where sufficient time is not available prior to the expiry of the Exploration Period to complete the appraisal work after the<br /><br />
Discovery, at the request of the Contractor, the Government shall extend the Exploration Period for such period, as may be<br /><br />
mutually determined by the Licensee and the Contractor, for the appraisal work to be carried out and for the Management<br /><br />
Committee, the Licensee, and the Contractor to comply with the provisions of Article 9 and Article 21, provided that the<br /><br />
period of extension shall not exceed (30) thirty contract months.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
2.10<br /><br />
<br /><br />
If no Commercial Discovery has been made in the Contract Area by the end of the Exploration Period, the Contract shall<br /><br />
terminate.<br /><br />

Force Majeure as defined in this Article.<br /><br />

31.2<br /><br />
<br /><br />
For the purpose of this Contract, the term Force Majeure means any cause or event, other than the unavailability of funds,<br /><br />
whether similar to or different from those enumerated herein, lying beyond the reasonable control of, and unanticipated or<br /><br />
unforeseeable by, and not brought about at the instance of. the Party claiming to be affected by such event, or which, if<br /><br />
anticipated or foreseeable, could not be avoided or provided for, and which has caused the non-performance or delay in<br /><br />
performance. Without limitation to the generality of the foregoing, the term Force Majeure shall include natural phenomena<br /><br />
or calamities, earthquakes, typhoons, fires, wars declared or undeclared, hostilities, invasions, blockades, riots, strikes,<br /><br />
insurrection and civil disturbances but shall not include the unavailability of funds.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
3l .3<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Where a Party is claiming suspension of its obligations on account of Force Majeure, it shall promptly, but in no case later<br /><br />
than seven (7) days after the occurrence of the event of Force Majeure, notify the other Parties in writing giving full<br /><br />
particulars of the Force Majeure, the estimated duration thereof, the obligations affected and the reasons for its suspension.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
31.4<br /><br />
<br /><br />
A Party claiming Force Majeure shall exercise reasonable diligence to seek to overcome the Force Majeure event and to<br /><br />
mitigate the effects thereof on the performance of its obligations under this Contract provided, however, that the settlement<br /><br />
of strikes or differences with employees shall be within the discretion of the Party having the difficulty. The Party affected<br /><br />
shall promptly notify the other Parties as soon as the Force Majeure event has been removed and no longer prevents it from<br /><br />
complying with the obligations which have been suspended and shall thereafter resume compliance with such obligations as<br /><br />
soon as possible.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
31.5<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The Party asserting the claim of Force Majeure shall have the burden of proving circumstances that constitute valid grounds<br /><br />
of Force Majeure under this Article and that such Party has exercised reasonable diligence and efforts to remedy the cause of<br /><br />
any alleged Force Majeure.<br /><br />

<br /><br />

ACCOUNTING PROCEDURE<br /><br />
SECTION 1<br /><br />
GENERAL PROVISIONS<br /><br />
Purpose<br /><br />
Generally, the purpose of this Accounting Procedure is to set out principles and procedures of accounting which will enable<br /><br />
the Government of India to monitor effectively the Contractor's costs, expenditures, production and income so that the<br /><br />
Government's entitlement to Profit Petroleum can be accurately determined pursuant to the terms of the Contract. More<br /><br />
specifically, the purpose of the Accounting Procedure is to:<br /><br />
<br /><br />
- classify costs and expenditures and to define which costs and expenditures shall be allowable for cost, production sharing<br /><br />
and participation purposes;<br /><br />
<br /><br />
- specify the manner in which the Contractor's shall be prepared and approved; and<br /><br />
<br /><br />
- address numerous other accounting related matters.<br /><br />
This Accounting Procedure is intended to apply to the provision of the Contract and is without prejudice to computation of<br /><br />
income tax under applicable revisions of the Income Tax Act, 1961, as amended.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Definitions<br /><br />
For purposes of this Accounting Procedure, the terms herein which are defined in the Contract shall the same meaning when<br /><br />
used in this Accounting Procedure.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Inconsistency<br /><br />
In the event of any inconsistency or conflict between provisions of this Accounting Procedure and the provisions of the<br /><br />
Contract, the other provisions of the Contract shall prevail.<br /><br />
